In-product help

Effective, contextual guidance that allows customers to manage and troubleshoot the product without leaving it

Key result

+28% findability

Background

Contextual, embedded assistance

In-product help offers step-by-step walkthroughs right inside the interface as customers work with cloud-based applications, so they don't have to leave the product to find answers.

It includes various types of content, such as getting started guides, release notes, and FAQs.

Challenge

High need, yet low satisfaction

A CSAT survey indicated that customers needed a more streamlined way to search for information due to deeply nested menus, and struggled to access content within the help panel because of its limited size.

Goal

Create clear pathways through multiple interaction modes, including search and browsing, to reduce customer effort.

Solution at a glance

A useful help tool providing real-time guidance

Impact

Results from in-depth customer interviews

+28% found it easier to find information
92% agreed the search functionality met their needs
88% found the table and image expand feature useful

Process behind

How we collaborated from ideation to launch

Scoping and planning

Prioritizing by customer research

Research found that 76% of the participants had needed or anticipated needing in-product help, and half of those who used it returned at least once a week — with Quick Help as their primary destination. Therefore, I decided to start with redesigning Quick Help.

Quick Help is where active customers look up instructions for task-based needs. Since many topics have long titles, I discussed this concern with engineers and proposed an auto-suggestion feature for search.

Evaluation

Testing the initial concepts

Usability testing with 6 participants surfaced two gaps: although the participants liked the expand-in-place feature for tables and figures, they were unsure how to navigate back to the previous state and topic list.

"The expand feature is good. You may miss the context if you have to scroll up and down."
"I would use the search function. It would be the easiest way to get to other content."

From there, I enhanced navigation with breadcrumbs and a back button, moved the topic list out of the hamburger menu, and improved the overall content design for the welcome page with the design system team.

Iteration

Validating ideas with internal stakeholders

The second iteration introduced a feedback widget, driven by both engineering and business benefits. The engineering team leveraged an existing Juniper website function to support data analysis.

I brainstormed and refined the design with input from Sales Engineers. For example, the “Start First Task” button was removed from the welcome page as it was not applicable beyond initial setup. The thumbs-down icon was also removed from the feedback flow due to its negative connotation.

Next steps

Where this could go next

Add links to relevant topics when customers indicate the current page is not helpful.

Searchable help content

Design guided tours that direct customers to the correct locations in the application to continue their tasks.

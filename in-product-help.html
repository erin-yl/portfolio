In-product help

Effective, contextual guidance that helps customers adopt and keep using the product — without leaving it.

Role

UX Designer/Engineer

Team

UX researcher, product manager, design system team, content strategist, engineers

Process

Research → scoping → prototype → usability study → launch

Key result

+28% findability

Background

Contextual help for task completion

In-product help offers step-by-step guidance right inside the interface as customers work with Security Director Cloud — so they don't have to leave the product to get unstuck.

Challenge

High need, yet low satisfaction

Customers needed a more efficient way to search for answers, and struggled most with locating and accessing content once they'd opened the help panel.

Goal: create intuitive, streamlined pathways for search and browsing, and increase customer satisfaction in usability studies and future surveys.

Scoping and planning

Prioritizing by customer research

Research showed that 76% of respondents had needed or anticipated needing in-product help, and half of those who used it returned at least once a week — with Quick Help as their primary destination. That data made the scoping decision clear: focus improvements where the impact would be highest.

Evaluation

Testing the initial concepts

Early usability testing validated the direction but surfaced two gaps: people liked the expand-in-place feature for tables and figures, but lost context scrolling through long articles; and the navigation system needed a clear way back to the topic list and previous state.

"The expand feature is good. You may miss the context if you have to scroll up and down."
"I would use the search function. It would be the easiest way to get to other content."

From there, I enhanced navigation with breadcrumbs and a back button, moved the topic list out of the hamburger menu, and reworked the welcome page to surface FAQs and content types more clearly.

Solution

A streamlined help tool for seamless product support

The final design gives customers a fast way in from any screen, clear pathways between the help panel, topic list, and articles, and a search experience with autosuggest so they rarely have to browse at all.

Impact

Results from in-depth customer interviews

+28% found it easier to find information
92% agreed the search functionality met their needs
88% found the table and image expand feature useful

Next steps

Where this could go next

Extend search functionality throughout in-product help, use feedback to link customers directly to relevant topics or the documentation site, and add contextual, dynamic tours that guide customers to the right place in the product.

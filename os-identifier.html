Operating system identifier

A thoughtfully designed differentiator of two network operating systems in one documentation ecosystem

Background

Why an identifier was needed

Junos OS and Junos OS Evolved (Evo) are two network operating systems that power Juniper's products. Although they run similarly, several features require different setup processes. However, their documentation lived side by side without a clear way to tell them apart.

Problem

Documentation was mixed up

Research finding

Product teams received customer complaints about Evo and Junos content being merged together, causing a drop in the satisfaction score.

Pain points

Customers couldn't tell which content applied to their OS, forcing them to manually filter out irrelevant information and spend extra time searching for the right details.

Goal

Provide an efficient browsing experience that differentiates Evo from Junos and highlights key features for each OS.

Solution at a glance

Coherent, color-coded documentation for each OS

Impact

Results from interviews and post-launch data

+60% found it easier to switch between the two OSes
100% agreed the new design met their needs
−5% bounce rate after launch

Process behind

How we collaborated from ideation to launch

Insights

Visual cues facilitate understanding

I collaborated with the brand team to select colors and logos aligned with the brand identity. Then, I determined how best to present these elements on documentation pages with the product team.

Survey responses from 48 participants and usability testing showed that these visual cues successfully helped customers recognize they were reading the correct documentation, but follow-up interviews surfaced three specific gaps.

  • 50% of participants couldn't find the dropdown menu for switching the OS.
  • Participants were unsure if the mixed content boxes were intentional or a mistake.
  • Participants were confused by the "Junos" label in the breadcrumbs on Evo pages.

That led to a rescope: completely separate Junos and Evo content, move the switch functionality to the top of the page, and display correct labels in breadcrumbs.

Iteration

Enhancing the switch functionality

I explored two directions for the OS switcher — a button pairing with a bold active state, and a toggle — before landing on a third option in a later round: a tab design that matched the mental model of switching between two related documents.

We also experimented with distinct banners and color schemes that reflected our branding. To improve accessibility, I proposed using high-contrast colors.

Next steps

Where this could go next

Identifiers in search results

Extend the identifier system to search results, so customers can tell which OS a result belongs to before clicking into it.

Scaling the pattern

Apply the same tab and color-coding pattern to other product pages (e.g., release notes, API docs) for consistency across the broader ecosystem.

Proactive OS detection

Explore proactive OS detection (e.g., based on account or device information) to reduce reliance on manual switching.

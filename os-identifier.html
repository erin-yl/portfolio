Operating system identifier

A thoughtfully designed differentiator of two network operating systems within a single documentation ecosystem

Role

Lead designer, team of 2

Team

Brand team

Product team

Software Engineer

Technical Writer

UX Researcher

Timeline

6 months

Key result

−5% bounce rate

Background

Why an identifier was needed

Junos OS and Junos OS Evolved (Evo) are two network operating systems that power Juniper's products. They run similarly, but require different setup processes — and their documentation lived side by side without a clear way to tell them apart.

Problem

Documentation was mixed up

Research finding

Product teams received negative user feedback about Evo and Junos documentation.

User pain points

Merged Evo and Junos content led to confusion and difficulty identifying the correct information.

Goal

Differentiate Evo from Junos and highlight relevant information for each OS, increasing customer satisfaction while decreasing bounce rate.

Solution at a glance

Coherent, color-coded documentation for each OS

The final design carries consistent tabs, colors, and headlines across every subsequent page, so customers always know which operating system's documentation they're reading — and can switch to the other in one click.

Impact

Results from interviews and post-launch data

+60% found it easier to switch between the two OSes
100% agreed the new design met their needs
−5% bounce rate after launch

Process behind

How we collaborated from ideation to launch

Insights

Visual cues facilitate understanding

Early testing with tags, logos, and color-coding showed these cues genuinely helped customers recognize they were reading the correct documentation — but follow-up interviews surfaced three specific gaps still tripping people up.

That led to a rescope: completely separate Junos and Evo content, update documentation titles, move the switch functionality to the top of the page, and show correct labels in breadcrumbs.

Switch functionality

Iterating on how to switch between OSes

I explored two directions for the OS switcher — a button pairing with a bold active state, and a toggle — before landing on a third option in a later round: a tab design that read clearly at a glance and matched the mental model of switching between two related documents.

Next steps

Where this could go next

Update the remaining product pages for consistency, and use what we learned here to improve the broader documentation structure.

Documentation redesign

An AI-powered support hub that gives customers easy access to the information they need

Role

Lead designer from concept to iteration

Team

Content Strategist

Sales Engineer

Software Engineer

Technical Writer

UX Researcher

Video Producer

Timeline

First phases: 4 months

Second phases: 6 months

Key result

+19% customer satisfaction

Background

A hard-to-navigate support hub

Documentation is where customers go for onboarding, troubleshooting, and ongoing learning. It is used daily by IT managers, network engineers, and system admins. The previous interface listed every resource as a long, undifferentiated list of links.

Problem

A customer survey showed declining satisfaction

Poor organization

Long, dense lists of links made it difficult to scan and find the right documentation.

Inconsistent categories

Inconsistent category naming across products made content less predictable and harder to navigate.

Goal: A logical content structure enhanced with conversational AI to help customers discover relevant information throughout their product journey.

Solution at a glance

A holistic, seamless support experience

Impact

Results from a survey and post-launch data

+21% found the hub easier to navigate
+24% increase in engagement and interest
−5% negative customer ratings after launch

Process behind

How we collaborated from ideation to launch

Phase 1 — IA and navigation

Streamlining the information architecture

I worked with a Content Strategist and Sales Engineer to categorize the documentation based on common user tasks.

The new structure shows a progressive journey from the initial setup to help resources. Customers could move from learning about a product to actively managing it, with each stage surfacing the content that mattered most at that point.

Phase 1 — IA and navigation

Initial design

Satisfaction score of 4.25/5

The new design scored 4.25 out of 5 in a usability testing with 8 participants. All participants responded positively to the well-organized layout, confirming the design was moving in the right direction.

Improvement

Enhance content variety — Analytics insights and internal interviews indicated demand for integrated video tutorials and training materials.

Reduce clicks — Usability study showed participants struggle to find specific resources when unfamiliar with technical terminology and want faster access.

Phase 2 — AI chatbot assistant

Designing an assistant customers could trust

Based on the findings, our team expanded the project scope by incorporating an AI assistant that enables users to dive deeper into content and complete complex tasks more efficiently. The design is guided by three principles: providing example questions, clarifying capabilities and limitations, and always returning verifiable source links.

The design defines five key states, including empty, loading, success, fallback, and feedback, to support both success and edge cases while ensuring user trust.

I also partnered with Content Strategists and Technical Writers to implement content tagging across documentation, improving AI processing and internal content management.

Takeaways

What I learned

Trustworthy, thoughtful AI

AI design builds trust through clear expectations, graceful error handling, and robust foundations.

Strategic focus & iteration

Effective AI features focus on specific use cases rather than trying to do everything at once.

The power of SMEs

Subject Matter Experts are valuable teammates for validating design ideas early and often.
